FILC Official Ranking System

The FILC World Rankings reflect player performance across a global calendar of sanctioned events in the sport of Arcade Rolling. Players earn points based on their final placement in each event, with each tournament categorized into one of five competitive tiers.

How Points Are Earned

Points are awarded for final placement only — not for perfect games, frame scores, or side achievements. A player's ranking is based on their top 10 finishes across the last 52 weeks of competition.

Event Tier Structure

  • Tier 1 — Majors (10,000-level): FILC Grand Championship events held annually at elite venues. Limited field. Global broadcast.
  • Tier 2 — 9K Series: Top-tier continental or legacy events (e.g., EuroRoll, Pacific Cup). Selective fields, high prestige.
  • Tier 3 — 5K Events: National championships and federation-hosted tournaments. Deep fields with tour implications.
  • Tier 4 — Open Tour: Sanctioned regional competitions open to all ranked players. Includes rising talent and tour veterans.
  • Tier 5 — Regional Circuit: Locally sanctioned amateur events. Feeder tournaments for emerging competitors.

Ranking Policy

Only a player's top 10 finishes are counted toward their current FILC ranking. Tiebreakers are resolved in the following order:

  1. Number of tour-level wins
  2. Highest finish in most recent head-to-head match
  3. Strength of best finish (weighted by event tier)

Rankings are updated weekly and reset annually at the close of the FILC Championship Masters.
